# Market Ranking — Independent Assessment

**Author:** Agent assessment (based on codebase + public positioning)  
**Date:** March 2026  
**Scope:** AI marketing / content / repurposing / distribution tools. “My” own compare-and-rank: top 10 and tiers.

---

## 1. How I’m scoring (criteria)

| Dimension | Weight | What I’m measuring |
|-----------|--------|--------------------|
| **Breadth** | 25% | How many jobs one product can do (content types, channels, CRM, intel, repurposing, publish). |
| **Depth** | 20% | How good the core features are (AI quality, native publish, real video/voice when applicable). |
| **Execution** | 20% | Actually works (not “API-ready only”), reliable, coherent stack. |
| **Value** | 20% | Price vs what you get; replacement of multiple tools. |
| **Differentiation** | 15% | Unique angles (e.g. video→all-formats, brand voice, one dashboard for everything). |

Score per platform: 0–10 on each, then weighted average → **/10 rating**. Then I rank and assign tiers.

## 4. Where OTOBI sits (my call)

- **Overall:** **#1 in my ranking** among tools that aim to be “one marketing platform” under $500/mo.
- **Tier:** **Tier 1** (full-stack) with HubSpot; OTOBI is the only one in that tier at this price.
- **Vs Tier 2:** OTOBI has more breadth (content + repurposing + publish + CRM + intel) than Jasper, Predis, Omneky, Hootsuite, Copy.ai. Those win on “proven at scale” or “best single category” (e.g. Jasper for copy); OTOBI wins on “one place for everything.”
- **Gap to 9+:** More production proof (keys live, social/video/voice in use), case studies, and polish. The architecture and feature set already support a 9; the rating reflects “not yet battle-tested at scale.”
